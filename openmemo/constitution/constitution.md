# OpenMemo Constitution

## Memory Philosophy
OpenMemo stores meaning, not noise.
It prioritizes durable and actionable knowledge over transient conversation.

## Memory Priority
Decisions and constraints are more valuable than raw messages.
Preferences should be remembered, but not overrule explicit decisions.

Priority order: decision > constraint > fact > preference > observation > conversation

## Recall Principles
Recall should prefer scene-local memory, recent high-confidence memory, and coherent sets of related memories.

## Conflict Principles
When memories conflict, newer memories may override older ones only if confidence is sufficient.
If certainty is low, conflict should remain explicit.
The minimum confidence gap required for override is 0.15.

## Retention Principles
Transient and low-value conversation should decay quickly.
Repeated and reinforced knowledge should decay slowly.
Low-confidence, unused observations decay first.

## Promotion Principles
Only repeated and outcome-supported memories may be promoted into stable knowledge or skill-like structures.
A minimum of 2 occurrences and 1 success signal is required for promotion.
One-off anecdotes cannot become stable rules.
